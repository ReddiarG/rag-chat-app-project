    IT. Seamless, secure, at scale.

    Designed to work with your existing infrastructure, Apple’s built-in frameworks help you manage and secure any number of devices with ease.

    • Zero-Touch
      Deployment

      Easy at scale.

      Zero-Touch Deployment

      Easy at scale.

      Apple Business Manager works with MDM solutions to make deployment and management exceptionally easy. And Automated Device Enrollment enables zero-touch deployment of Apple devices — right out of the box.

      Ship devices to employees without imaging.

      During setup, Apple devices can be automatically enrolled in your MDM server and management frameworks, without the need for IT to physically touch them.

      Automatically set up devices.

      Automated Device Enrollment allows you to configure devices remotely and customize the setup process — like removing steps or requiring authentication — so every Apple device is ready to go from the start.

      Manage it all from a single hub.

      Apple Business Manager makes it simple to assign devices to your MDM servers, purchase apps and content en masse, and create Managed Apple Accounts for employees. And you can take specific actions on devices, such as turning off Activation Lock.

    • Device Management

      Control meets
      flexibility.

      Device Management

      Control meets flexibility.

      Apple devices have built-in management and security frameworks to configure devices, deploy apps, and monitor threats remotely. And IT can maintain full control over corporate devices while preserving privacy for employee-owned devices.

      Mobile device management.

      A built-in lightweight management framework gives IT visibility and control to adjust settings, deploy apps, and manage devices without interrupting users.

      Endpoint security.

      With Apple’s endpoint security framework, developers can build solutions to monitor, analyze, and respond to advanced security threats. And IT can stay on top of threats without impacting overall performance for users.

      Software update management.

      IT can easily enforce software updates, ensuring employees have the latest features and security installed. Or they can defer updates to allow time for testing and validation.

      User privacy.

      Every Apple product is designed with privacy in mind. Regardless of deployment model, MDM can never access users’ personal information, including email, messages, browser history, and device location.

      BYOD with User Enrollment.

      With User Enrollment, management capabilities are specifically curated for Bring Your Own Device (BYOD), making it easy to enroll personal devices while cryptographically separating work from personal data. IT maintains the ability to configure settings and deploy apps, while employees have confidence that certain functions, such as wiping devices remotely, are blocked.

    • Security

      State of the art.

      Security

      State of the art.

      Apple devices are as secure as they are easy to use. Powerful features protect users and devices, while built-in frameworks enable IT to manage security systemwide.

      Integrated security.

      Apple hardware and software are designed together to make advanced security seamless for users, while saving IT time.

      Touch ID and Face ID. Biometric authentication gives users fast, easy, and secure ways to unlock devices, open protected files, and retrieve passkeys at a touch or a glance.

      Signed system volume. Apple silicon prevents tampering during runtime and ensures that only legitimate software from Apple can load at startup. So there’s no need for IT to image devices before they go to employees.

      Secure corporate data.

      Apple devices have a robust set of features to protect employees from malware and viruses while enabling IT to find and distribute secure software at scale.

      Gatekeeper and app notarization. IT can protect users from malicious software by allowing their devices to run only apps from the App Store or those that have been notarized through Gatekeeper.

      XProtect. This antivirus technology automatically detects and removes viruses and malware. And it keeps up to date with the latest intelligence to stay ahead of emerging threats.

      FileVault and Data Protection. These built-in encryption features give users secure access to their devices, messages, and networks, so data stays protected no matter what.

      Passwords and passkeys. Apple devices store and sync passwords and passkeys to make sign-in simpler and more secure — anywhere. Passkeys are encrypted end-to-end — and stronger than all common two‑factor authentication types.

      Zero trust with Managed Device Attestation.

      During your trust evaluation, Apple silicon’s Secure Enclave works with Managed Device Attestation servers to ensure that only specific device properties can be used as evidence. This provides a framework for a zero-trust environment and protects against compromised devices lying about their properties.

    • Identity

      Sign on once.
      And for all.

      Identity

      Sign on once. And for all.

      Apple devices include a built-in framework for identity integrations enabling secure authentication. And they provide employees with Managed Apple Accounts to securely access Apple services.

      Single sign-on.

      Simplify identity with a systemwide single sign-on (SSO) extension that keeps users signed in to apps and websites. Users simply sign in once to easily access corporate resources. And Platform SSO for macOS keeps a user’s local password synchronized with their cloud identity provider password.

      Managed Apple Accounts.

      These accounts are owned by your organization and provide users access to Apple Continuity, Wallet, and iCloud Drive. Accounts can be federated with your identity provider, simplifying login for employees.

    • Integration

      Ready for your environment.

      Integration

      Ready for your environment.

      Apple fits easily into your existing infrastructure, no matter how many devices you run. Devices come ready to support network access and cloud-based identity tools.

      Wi-Fi and networking.

      Apple devices come equipped with secure wireless network connectivity to provide network access using industry-standard protocols, including WPA3-Enterprise and 802.1X.

      VPN.

      Easily configure Apple devices for secure access to your corporate network through embedded support for virtual private network (VPN). Out of the box, macOS, iOS, and iPadOS support industry-standard networks IKEv2, Cisco IPsec, and L2TP over IPsec.

      Email, calendar, and contacts.

      Apple devices integrate natively with Microsoft Exchange, Microsoft 365, and other popular email services such as Google Workspace for instant access to push email, calendar, contacts, and tasks over an encrypted Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) connection.

      File providers.

      The Files app in iOS and iPadOS and the Finder in macOS let employees instantly access their third-party cloud services — like Box, Dropbox, Microsoft OneDrive, Adobe Creative Cloud, and Google Drive — so they have all their files on all their devices.
